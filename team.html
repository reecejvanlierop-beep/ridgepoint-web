Sports Medicine

Dr. Eric Anderson, MD

Sports Medicine Physician

Dr. Anderson leads the clinical foundation at RidgePoint as our sports medicine physician. Every patient who walks through our doors begins with his comprehensive evaluation — ensuring the right diagnosis is established before any treatment begins.

His approach is rooted in non-operative excellence. Dr. Anderson specializes in treating the full spectrum of musculoskeletal conditions — from acute sports injuries and tendinopathies to chronic joint pain and spine complaints — with a conservative-first philosophy that exhausts every viable option before considering more invasive interventions.

Patients consistently describe Dr. Anderson as thorough, patient, and direct. He takes the time to explain what's happening in plain language, set realistic expectations, and build care plans that are grounded in evidence and aligned with each patient's goals.

"The right diagnosis changes everything. We can't build a meaningful plan without it."

Dr. Robert Friendt, DC

Doctor of Chiropractic

Dr. Friendt brings over a decade of clinical experience to RidgePoint, including years working directly alongside orthopedic surgeons and sports medicine physicians in the Houston Medical Center — one of the largest and most complex medical environments in the United States. That background shaped his clinical precision, his collaborative instincts, and his ability to function within a high-stakes, team-based care environment.

A former Division I pole vaulter and decathlete at the University of Minnesota, Dr. Friendt understands athletic performance and injury from the inside out. He earned his Doctorate from Texas Chiropractic College and built his clinical career treating elite athletes, surgical patients, and complex MSK cases alongside orthopedic and sports medicine teams.

Dr. Friendt's practice is grounded in hands-on manual therapy and corrective exercise — not spinal manipulation or adjustments. He uses precise soft tissue techniques to address the underlying drivers of pain and dysfunction, and pairs that work with corrective exercise so results carry forward between sessions. His training includes the Fascial Distortion Model, which informs how he assesses and interprets each patient's presentation.

"The body tells you exactly what it needs — if you know how to listen to how a patient describes their pain."

Rehabilitation

Reece Van Lierop

Rehab Specialist

A Spokane native, Reece earned his degree in Applied Kinesiology from Corban University where he developed a deep foundation in human movement, biomechanics, and exercise science. He joined RidgePoint with a clear vision: to build a clinic where rehabilitation is done right — grounded in clinical reasoning, connected to a physician-led team, and focused on functional outcomes that actually matter to patients.

Reece specializes in bridging the critical gap between receiving a diagnosis and achieving long-term function. He builds progressive, individualized rehab programs based on each patient's movement patterns, goals, and clinical presentation — not templates. His patients consistently report that he listens carefully, explains things clearly, and pushes them to achieve more than they thought possible.

Outside the clinic, Reece stays active and maintains a deep connection to the Spokane community — which shapes how he understands what staying active really means to the people he works with.

"Getting out of pain is the start. Building the capacity to stay out of pain — that's the real work."
